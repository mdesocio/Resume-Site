Welcome!

About Me!

During my early years of grade school, I could remember times where I would come home, and just tinker with whatever was on my dad’s workbench at the time. An old computer that was being updated and replaced. A network switch with a bad fan in place. Regardless of what it was, I thought it was cool! Of course, when you’re 4 or 5 you don’t ever know what these do other than you can play games on the old CRT monitor, “…but don’t touch daddy’s computer!” Progressively through my middle and high school careers, I began exploring what these technologies could do. It really started when my dad gave me my first NAS around age 13. This was the steppingstone that allowed me to design my rack. I saved for years, then depleted part of my savings so I could buy a bass for music school, but after I threw in the towel on a B.A. in Music Education at my dream school at the time, I knew I had a lot of learning to do to make sure I was on top of my game. Sure, you don’t need this equipment to start a degree in Information Technology, but if you want to expand your knowledge past a textbook and into a more real-world environment, this is pretty much one of two options: Cloud or on premises. Of course, hybrid is also an option too. For me, data flow and network design is my passion. Figuring out how the network will be segregated, how the backbones will link back to the core switch or router, L2 or L3 switching and where each is necessary, and many other pieces that I don’t know yet are what excite me. As the requirements for increased speed and lower latency keep coming back, I see a small timeline before 25Gb/s and even 50Gb/s is a must for most homes and small businesses, as every service will be cloud based or decentralized on a blockchain across the world!
